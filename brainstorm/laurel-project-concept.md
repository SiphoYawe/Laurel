# LAUREL: Project Concept Document

> **30 Days to Academic Transformation**

---

## Executive Summary

**Laurel** is an AI-powered daily coaching companion that transforms university students' academic resolutions into measurable results through science-backed habits and evidence-based learning methods.

Built for the **"Commit To Change: An AI Agents Hackathon"**, Laurel directly answers the hackathon's core challenge: *"Build AI that turns New Year's resolutions into real results."*

---

## The Problem

University students face three critical gaps:

| Gap | Description | How Students Experience It |
|-----|-------------|---------------------------|
| **Execution Gap** | Know what to do, can't make themselves do it | "I know I should use active recall, but I just... don't" |
| **Clarity Gap** | Overwhelmed by options, don't know where to start | "There's so much advice out there, what actually works?" |
| **Persistence Gap** | Start strong, fall off after weeks | "I was doing great in September, but by October..." |

---

## Target User

**Primary:** University/college students who:
- Set academic goals but struggle to follow through
- Want to improve grades but don't know the best methods
- Start each semester with good intentions that fade

## Daily Interaction Model

### Morning Check-in (2-3 minutes)
```
Laurel: "Good morning! Day 12 of your journey to ace Organic Chemistry.

Today's focus: Your first active recall session on Chapter 5.
Remember - struggle is good! When retrieval feels hard, that's
your brain building stronger pathways.

Your habit stack for today:
â˜• After morning coffee â†’ Open Orgo notes â†’ 10 min active recall

What's one thing that might get in the way today?"

Student: "I have a group project meeting at the time I usually study"

Laurel: "Good catch! Let's plan around it. Can you do a quick
5-minute recall session before the meeting instead? Even micro-sessions
count. The two-minute rule: just open the notes. Once you start,
you'll likely do more."
```

### Evening Reflection (2-3 minutes)
```
Laurel: "How did today go with your active recall session?"

Student: "I only did 5 minutes before the meeting, but I actually
did another 15 minutes after dinner"

Laurel: "That's a WIN! You adapted instead of skipping entirely.
That's the 'never miss twice' principle in action - you showed up
even when the schedule changed.

What did you recall from Chapter 5? Tell me one thing without
looking at your notes."

Student: "Um... the SN1 mechanism involves a carbocation intermediate"

Laurel: "Perfect! You just did active recall right now. That
moment of struggle before you remembered? That strengthened the
memory.

Tomorrow we'll do a quick check on Chapter 5 again - that's
spaced repetition working for you.

Sleep well - your brain will consolidate what you learned today!"
```

## Opik Integration Strategy

### The Three-Layer Approach

#### Layer 1: Quality (Prove the coach is GOOD)

| Metric | What It Measures | Target |
|--------|-----------------|--------|
| Dialogue Helpfulness | Is each response actually useful? | >90% helpful |
| User Frustration | Are we annoying or stressing students? | <5% frustration signals |
| Session Completeness | Did each check-in achieve its goal? | >85% complete |
| Answer Relevance | Are responses on-topic? | >95% relevant |

#### Layer 2: Optimization (Show the coach gets BETTER)

| Feature | How We Use It | Demo Value |
|---------|---------------|------------|
| HRPO Optimizer | Auto-improve coaching prompts based on helpfulness scores | Before/after comparison |
| Prompt Versioning | Track which coaching approaches work best | Show iteration history |
| Failure Analysis | Identify where coaching fails and why | Root cause insights |
| A/B Testing | Test different prompt strategies | Data-driven decisions |

#### Layer 3: Outcomes (Prove it actually WORKS)

| Metric | What It Measures | Demo Value |
|--------|-----------------|------------|
| Resolution Progress | % movement toward stated goal | Transformation story |
| Habit Formation | Number of habits that became automatic | Behavioral change proof |
| Journey Completion | % of students finishing 30 days | Engagement validation |
| Qualityâ†’Outcome Correlation | Does better coaching = better results? | The ultimate proof |
